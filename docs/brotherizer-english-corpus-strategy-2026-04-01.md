# Brotherizer English Corpus Strategy

Date: 2026-04-01

## First target

Build the first donor memory around:

- English
- British English first
- then broader worldwide online English

The goal is not "formal English."
The goal is:

- internet-native English,
- comment/reply style,
- irony,
- casual disagreement,
- shortform persuasion,
- slang and understatement,
- audience adaptation.

## Best first buckets

### Bucket 1: British English

Prioritize:

- UK commentary
- UK podcast clips
- UK music/fashion/pop-culture comments
- football banter
- British reaction humor

Why:

- strong identity markers,
- different understatement/irony patterns,
- good anti-generic signal,
- distinctive lexical rhythm.

### Bucket 2: Worldwide online English

Prioritize:

- YouTube comments on commentary/podcast clips
- gaming discourse
- internet drama / pop culture
- music audiences
- creator audience replies

Why:

- gives Brotherizer broader internet-native English,
- prevents overfitting into only one British voice,
- improves general "sounds like a real person online" behavior.

## Do not over-index on

- corporate English
- polished newsletter voice
- SEO blog tone
- academic English
- heavily scripted creator monologues
- children's content comments

## Good early query seeds

- `UK commentary reaction podcast clips`
- `British humour podcast clips`
- `UK music reaction interview clips`
- `Premier League podcast clips banter`
- `internet drama commentary clips`
- `gaming hot takes funny clips podcast`
- `pop culture commentary internet discourse`
- `creator reaction comments english`

## Metadata Brotherizer should preserve

- `lang = en / en-GB`
- `region_code = GB / US / AU / CA`
- `topic_tags`
- `audience_mode = comment-like / reply-like`
- style features from chunker

## Product reason

This first English corpus should teach Brotherizer:

- British understatement
- online irony
- less symmetrical sentence rhythm
- punchier landings
- more natural shortform disagreement
- more human comment-thread phrasing
